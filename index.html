Asthma Alley: Mott Haven for Green Spaces

The Cross Bronx Expressway is infamous for the environmental havoc it has wreaked upon the residents that live near it due to the many FreshDirect food trucks as well as other delivery trucks that use the expressway.

The neighborhood of Mott Haven is so highly impacted by the pollution that it’s nicknamed ‘Asthma Alley’. An article by the Guardian reports that 97% of the residents in this neighborhood are Hispanic or Black. Christopher Tessum conducted a study for the National Academy of Sciences about the racial disparities of the pollution in Mott Haven and found that Black Americans ‘are exposed to about 56% more pollution than is caused by their consumption and Hispanics 63% more’ while non-hispanic whites breathe about 17% less air pollution than they cause.

Even though the South Bronx is surrounded by rivers VICE reports that children who live there don’t get to experience those rivers and some don’t even know that there are rivers that are close to them. The multiple expressways intersecting through each other create a barrier for the children as well as increase their risks of health disparities. The urgency to fix this problem is higher now because of COVID-19. Since the novel virus attacks the respiratory system, people with pre-existing conditions are more at risk.

Movements to increase greenspaces in cities are growing more so now than ever before.
