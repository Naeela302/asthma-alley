The Bronx is currently a “non-attainment area” for particulate matter (PM2.5), which means that the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s current annual standard for this pollutant is exceeded.

Past land use planning decisions in the South Bronx have placed this sensitive population in close proximity to highways, truck routes, industrial land use areas and other environmental hazards. Studies have shown that traffic associated with these facilities exacerbates asthma symptoms.
